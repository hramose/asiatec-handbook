Guidelines on Course Dropping

Section 1

Dropping of subjects may be allowed provided that it does not exceed twenty percent (20%) of the students total course load; it shall be done in writing; with the consent of the Dean/Subject Teacher concerned and Guidance Counselor. This should be done not later than one week after the classes have started.

Section 2

Any student who continues to be absent from his/her classes beyond the last day of dropping of courses and exceeds the maximum number of allowable absences shall be given a failing grade with remarks of excessive absences or Failure Due to Absences (FDA).

Section 3

The following procedure will guide the students in the dropping of courses:

3.1. Secure a dropping form from the Registrar’s Office

3.2. See the faculty concerned to seek his consent/advice and signature.

3.3. Proceed to the Cashier for payment

3.4. Submit the accomplished dropping form to the Registrar’s Office

Section 4

When a student registers in the school, it is understood that he/she is enrolling for the entire short term. A “term” shall mean short-term for non degree courses and entire term or semester for degree courses.

Section 5

Refund Policy. A student who transfers or withdraws in writing within two (2) weeks after the beginning of the classes and who has already paid the prescribed tuition and other school fees in full or/for any length longer than one month, shall be charged twenty percent (20%) or if the student withdraws within the first week of classes, ten percent (10%) of the total amount due for the term. Student shall be charged all the school fees in full if he/she withdraws after the second week of classes.

Graduation

Section 1

A Candidate for Graduation should have followed the guidelines below before he/she can graduate:

1.1 Only the Students who had successfully completed all courses/subjects required in his/her curricula is eligible for graduation.


1.2 Student expecting to graduate should also meet the unit percentage requirements:


1.2.1 Fifty percent (50%) of the totalnumber of units required for the course


1.2.2 Seventy-five percent (75%) of the Major areas should have been completed at ASIATECH.


1.3. Prospective candidate for graduation must file his/her application for graduation at the Office of the Registrar not later than one (1) month after the start of the term/semester.


1.4 A student cannot participate in the Commencement Exercise/Graduation Rites unless all requirements in the curriculum and department clearance have been complied.


1.5 After the graduation, diploma can be claimed from the Registrar’s office within six (6) months upon completion of all requirements for the program.

Section 2.Candidates for with Honors.

Candidate for graduation who will be receiving honors shall have any of the following General Weighted Average:

2.1 Suma cum Laude - - - - - - - 1.10 to 1.00


2.2 Magna cum Laude - - - - - - 1.20 to 1.11


2.3 Cum Laude - - - - - - - - - - - 1.30 to 1.21 - Where Suma Cum Laude can avail free tour aboard.


2.4 With Honors - - - - - - - - - - 1.31 to 1.50


2.5 Academic Achiever - - - - - 1.51 to 1.70


2.2 The Guidelines on graduation with honors shall be as follows


2.2.1 A student’s final grades during his/her last school term shall be submitted 30-days before the graduation


2.2.2 Only final grades shall be considered in the computation of the general weighted average


2.2.3 In the computation of the final averages of a candidate for graduation with honors, grades in all accredited academic subjects in the curriculum shall be included


2.2.2 Every candidate for graduation with honors must

a. Have carried the normal load prescribed in his/her curriculum, except in the last semester

b. Have completed in the School at least 75% of the total number of the academic units or hours required for graduation

c. Have been residence for at least 3 years immediately prior to Graduation

d. Have no final grade lower than 2.25 and/or Incomplete in any Academic subject whether prescribed or not in his curriculum which he/she has taken in the School, or in any other educational institution

f. Have not repeated a subject in other educational institution

Student Discipline

Section 1

ASIATECH Student Discipline Board/Grievance Committeeshall be composed of the following:

1.1 The Head of Student Affairs as its chairman/Guidance and Counselling officer

1.2 Dean for academic Affairs/Department Heads.

1.3 A representative of the faculty organization duly designated.

1.4 The President of the supreme Student Council.

 Section 2

Power and Functions The Student Discipline Board is mandated by the provision of this handbook to convene itself at the call of its Chairman not later than three school days after a written complaint has filed before the Office of the Students. The Student Discipline Board performs the following functions:

3.1 Investigates major violations of students in student handbook

3.2 Determines and imposes appropriate Sanction of the offenses

3.3 Determines, formulates and applies re-entry program for the offenders in the light of the offense and if the sanction given is maximum suspension dismissal or expulsion

3.4 Creates a committee to a further investigate a case if necessary, and makes final decision on the matter.

4.3.2 Cheating or academic dishonesty.

The following acts shall be considered forms of cheating or academic dishonesty.

a. Possession or passing of notes or any examination-related (materials) during examination period in any means.

b. Facilitating/aiding in the dissemination of leakage.

c. Buying or selling of test papers of any portion thereof

d. Copying from one’s test paper or allowing others to copy from own examination paper

e. Having somebody else to take the examination in his/her behalf

f. Talking with others during examinations

g. Other similar acts that may be determined by the Board of Discipline.


First offense is minimum sanction of 3-day suspension to a maximum of dismissal.


4.3.3 . Forgery, falsification, tampering or alteration of official document of the school.

- First offense is Dismissal. In addition, students who forged, falsified, tampered, or altered any official document of the school shall be blacklisted. The school shall no longer issue any document, record, or certificate in his favor. This implies even after the student has already transferred to other school or has graduated from that school.


Section 5.Procedure of Grievance:

5.1 For Minor Offenses

5.1.1 The person-in-authority or his/her agent shall inform the student of the offense commited.

5.1.2 The apprehending person shall confiscate the ID of the erring student. The ID, with the report of the offense, shall be turned over to the Office of the Students Affairs.

5.1.3 The head of OSA shall notify the student in writing of the charge against him/her and shall give him/her opportunity to answer the same.

5.1.4 Sanction, if called for, shall be given in writing.

5.2 For Major Offenses

5.2.1 The aggrieved party or the Dean of the College or his representative shall submit formal complaint to the Office of Students Affairs.

5.2.2 The head of OSA shall convene the Board of Discipline to act on the complaint.

5.2.3 The board shall receive evidence from both parties.

5.2.4 The board shall deliberate and investigate on the evidence presented and prepares a recommendation.

5.2.5 The board shall forward the recommendation for sanction to the School Director for approval.

5.2.6 The recommendation shall be subjected to the approval of the School President.

Student Organization

Section 1. Recognized Student Organization

1.1. Recognition

1.1.1. A recognized student organization is any association, club, council or any form of group of perform students granted permission to Function within ASIATECH by the Office of the School President upon the recommendation of the Office of the Student Affairs and other concerned departments.

1.1.2.All recognized student organization shall have their constitution and by-laws duly ratified by their members. The constitution and by-laws shall be in consonance with the philosophy and advocacy of the school and must not be contrary to law. It shall also be approved by the Office of Student Affairs.

1.1.3Membership in any recognized student organization shall be strictly limited to the bona fide students of ASIATECH.

1.1.4. The officers of the student organizations shall be elected by the members subject to the provision of their respective constitution and by-laws.


1.2. Qualifications of an adviser

1.2.1. Preferably in the area of expertise related to the activities of the organization.

1.2.2.Must be a full-time/Part-time faculty member of ASIATECH.

POLICY AND PROCEDURES FOR STUDENT’S CLEARANCE

Section 1

School Policy. Securing student clearance is required for all graduating students and those who decided to officially drop. Clearance is also required if the student is leaving the school for transfer purposes and shall be a pre-requisite before an honorable dismissal from can be issued. The student should be cleared of all financial and property accountabilities from the different departments/units of the school before a diploma, transcript of records, certification, will be issued to him/her.

Section 2

Procedures. Here is the step-by-step process in signing of clearance.


2.1.The student is advised to secure the Student’s Clearance Form from the Registrar’s Office.

2.2.After filling out the form, the student is required to see all department heads or authorized signatories as listed in the form, for their signatures.

2.3.Once the accountability has been settled and signatures required in the clearance have been completed, the student shall file the same to the registrar Office.

2.4.The student pays at the Cashier’s Office unsettled balance that is necessary before the release of the documents being requested.

Section 3

Semestral Clearance


3.1.Repeat procedure 2.1-2.4 stated above

3.2.Present the accomplished clearance form to the Cashier’s Office for purpose of securing permit slip and permit enroll in next term/semester.
