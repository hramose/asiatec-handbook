WELCOME TO THE ASIA TECHNOLOGICAL SCHOOL OF SCIENCE AND ARTS

Foreword


This Asiatech Student Handbook contains information applicable to all bona fide students of Asia Technological School of Science and Arts who are enrolled in any degree or non-degree programs offered by the school and who, upon admission agree to obey the same.

The foundation of the set of policies, rules and regulations contained herein are all inspired from the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) and Technical Education and skills Development Authority (TESDA) memoranda which cannot be amended or revised without proper consultation with the different sectors of the academe subject for approval by the Board of Trustees.

Aside from this handbook, an enrolled student is also obliged to refer to other means of information within the school such as bulletin boards, school papers, departmental memoranda and others.

Furthermore, contents indicated herein may be subjected to change through proper amendment or additional procedures. Changes subsequent to the publication of this handbook shall follow and be implemented as the need and circumstances may arise.

Section 4. History

On June 9, 2009, ASIATECH originally APTECH eventually opened its first classes. The plan of the founder, Mrs. Marife L. Barraquio was to build a pre-school as she visualized that her owned commercial building could be utilized as a potential school because its accessibility and its location is very strategic, but due the urged of one of his Mentor Mr. Daniel N. Arriola to built a Tertiary School instead of a Pre-school, the plan was indeed changed. During that time, Mr. Arriola is in need of work and he believed that Mrs. Barraquio could be an answer, since she is known to be kind and generous. Indeed, she supported Mr. Ariola then APTECH was founded (The name before ASIATECH). APTECH stands for Asia Pacific technical School of Science and Arts. Historically, the owner Mr. Noel N. Barraquio and Mrs. Marife L. Barraquio has given their full trust to Mr. Ariola to manage the School because they believe that trusting people would make them effective and fruitful. The school is credited with less than 50 pioneering students and set of Faculty and staff. The only offered courses were TESDA courses such as Computer Electronic Technology, 2-year Information Technology and 2-yr Hotel and Restaurant Management. After two years in operation, On April 2011, the owner decided to personally operate the School. They added CHED courses such as Bachelor of Science in Hotel and Restaurant Management, Bachelor of Secondary Education major in English, Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, Bachelor of Science in Information Technology and Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. And additional TESDA courses such as SMAW and 1-year Nursing Aide.

CLASSIFICATION OF UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS

Section 1 The following guidelines shall be applied in classifying students according year level:

1.1 Freshmen Students are those who have finished 25 percent or less of the total number of units required for the degree or program feeling taken;

1.2 Sophomore Students are those who have completed not less than 26 percent but not more than 50 percent of the total number of units required for the course being taken;

1.3 Junior Students are those who have earned not less than 501 percent of the number of units required of the entire program and;

1.4 Senior Students are those who have successfully passed the prescribed courses of the first, second and third years of the curricular program being taken.

Section 2. Students are also classified as regular and irregular students. Regular students are those who completed the required subjects for the semesters already taken as indicated in the curricular schedule otherwise, the students are deemed irregular.

Section 3. Grading Parameters. Grades shall be based on the major exam per grading period added to the Class Standing that involves: Quizzes, recitation/seatworks, reports/assignments, and attendance.

Section 4. Grading Formulas. The following formulas are applied for the averaging system of grading that should be followed

Prelim period

Prelim Exam (40%) + Prelim Class Standing (60%)

= Prelim Grade (100%)

 Midterm Period

Midterm Exam (40%) + Midterm Class Standing (60%)

= Midterm Grade (100%)

 Pre-final Period

Pre-final Exam (40%) + Pre-final Class Standing (60%)

= Pre-final Grade (100%)

 Final Period

Final Exam (40%) + Final Class Standing (60%)

= Final Grade (100%)

COMPUTATION OF GENERAL AVERAGE GRADE

Prelim Midterm Pre-final Final General Grade + Grade + Grade + Grade Average

Section 5. Special Classes. In case of special classes like tutorial and summer classes, the percentage distribution indicated above for Class Standing and the formula for every period are in use.

Prelim Grade + Final Grade = General Average for 2

A Base 60 Transmutation table is provided by the School Administration to each faculty member to identify easily the equivalent percentage of raw scores depending on the number of items in any educational parameters mentioned in Section 3 of Article VI.

Guidelines on Attendance,Absences, and Examination

Section 1

Prompt and Regular Attendance. Every Student is expected to observe prompt and regular attendance from the first meeting of every course onwards.

Section 2

Maximum Allowable Absences. The maximum allowable number of absences for a student, per semester is twenty percent (20%) of the total number of hours devoted for a course.

Section 3

A student will be given a grade of 5.0 if he/she incurs more than the allowable number of absences.

Section 4

A student is considered absent if he/she arrive after:

4.1 the first 15 minutes for a one-hour class

4.2 the first 25 minutes for a one and Half Hour class4.3 the first 35 minutes for a two-hour class

4.4 the first 45 minutes for a three-hour class

Section 5

A student who is considered late or tardy will receive one third (1/3) absence if he/she arrives in class within the time frame mentioned in section 4. Three (3) lates shall be counted as one absence.

Section 6

Although members of the faculty are expected to begin their classes promptly, various contingencies which are sometimes unavoidable may cause some faculty members to be late in attending their Classes. In Such cases the following guidelines are to be observed:

6.1 Students should not leave the classroom until after (15) minutes have passes for one-hour class

6.2 Students are expected to remain quiet so as not to disturb others conducting classes while waiting for their instructor in the classroom.

Section 7

Excused/Justified Absences. Excused absences are absences that entitle students to make-up for missed classroom activities and are not counted against the.

Section 8

Absences due to co-curricular activities must be duly authorized in writing by the Coordinator/ Dean of the College and endorsed by the Office of student Affairs.

Section 9

The student is held responsible in securing the necessary excuse slip from the office of the Student affairs.

Section 10

Absences of Late Enrollees. Absences of late enrollees should be counted from the first meeting day after the semester.

Section 11

Absences during Term Examination will be considered only if due to the following cases:

11.1 Sickness

11.2 Conflict with other subjects

11.3 Other cases which are unavoidable in nature

Section 12

Special Examinations. Students who failed to take the term examination due to the reasons enumerated in section 11 of this given special examination provided that they will pay the corresponding special examination fee to be determined by the school
